# Logistics Services Agreement

This Logistics Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of February 20, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Stellar Logistics Corp.**, a New Jersey corporation ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on February 19, 2027.

## 1. Services

Provider shall provide warehousing and freight services as described in the applicable Statement of Work.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party from third-party claims arising out of (a) breach of this Agreement, (b) gross negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) any third-party intellectual property infringement claim relating to the Indemnifying Party's deliverables (subject to the standard exclusions for modifications, combinations, and out-of-scope use). The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party, grant sole control of defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section is the sole remedy for IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA. Each Party's total cumulative liability shall not exceed the greater of (A) amounts paid by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

The Recipient shall protect the Discloser's Confidential Information with at least the same care it uses for its own and no less than reasonable care, use it solely to perform hereunder, restrict access to need-to-know personnel under equivalent obligations, and not disclose to third parties without consent. Standard exclusions apply. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice. Obligations survive five (5) years; trade-secret protection is perpetual.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other Party materially breaches and **fails to cure such breach within seven (7) days** of receiving written notice. Either Party may also terminate for cause if the other becomes insolvent or files bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases business. Upon termination, surviving sections survive, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date and continues as specified above. This Agreement shall not automatically renew.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive relief to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor) may, on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and not more than once in any twelve (12) month period, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing limitations do not apply.
