# Consulting Master Services Agreement

This Consulting Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of November 12, 2024 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Gamma Industries LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on November 11, 2027.

## 1. Services

Provider shall perform consulting services described in mutually executed Statements of Work referencing this Agreement.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from any third-party claims, judgments, losses, liabilities, damages, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of (a) the Indemnifying Party's breach of this Agreement, (b) gross negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) any claim that the Indemnifying Party's deliverables, used as authorized, infringe the third party's intellectual property rights. The IP indemnity excludes (i) modifications not made by the Indemnifying Party, (ii) combinations with non-provided materials, and (iii) use outside the scope of this Agreement. As conditions of indemnity, the Indemnified Party shall promptly notify, grant sole control of defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section is the sole remedy for third-party IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION, (ii) CONFIDENTIALITY BREACH, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) FEES OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOST DATA. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF (A) FEES PAID UNDER THE APPLICABLE SOW IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM, OR (B) ONE HUNDRED THOUSAND U.S. DOLLARS ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

Each Party shall protect the other's Confidential Information using at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance, and no less than reasonable care. Confidential Information shall be used only as needed to perform under this Agreement, disclosed only to personnel with a need to know who are bound by equivalent obligations, and not disclosed to third parties without consent. Standard exclusions (already known, publicly available, third-party lawfully received, independently developed) apply. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to and cooperation with the Discloser. Obligations survive five (5) years post-termination, with trade-secret protections perpetual.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days (no cure for confidentiality, IP, or late payment), (b) becomes insolvent or enters bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (c) ceases business. Upon termination, surviving sections survive, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, and Provider shall make any Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date. This Agreement may renew for additional twelve (12) month terms; renewal requires affirmative written consent of both Parties via a renewal SOW signed prior to the expiration of the then-current term. Renewal pricing shall not increase by more than the lesser of five percent (5%) or the change in the U.S. CPI-U over the same period. Either Party may prevent renewal by notice at least sixty (60) days before the end of the then-current term.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts of Delaware. Either Party may seek injunctive or equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor under equivalent confidentiality protection) may, once per twelve (12) months on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Audits shall be during business hours, of reasonable scope, and shall not unreasonably interfere with Provider's operations. Provider may satisfy this obligation with a current SOC 2 Type II report or ISO 27001 attestation. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing notice and frequency limits do not apply.
