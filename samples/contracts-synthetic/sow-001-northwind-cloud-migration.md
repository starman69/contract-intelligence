# Statement of Work No. 001 — Cloud Migration Phase 1

This Statement of Work No. 001 (the "SOW") is entered into as of April 1, 2026 (the "SOW Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation** ("Customer") and **Northwind Systems Inc.** ("Provider"). This SOW is governed by and incorporated into the **Master Services Agreement dated March 15, 2026** between Customer and Provider (the "MSA"). Capitalized terms used and not defined herein have the meanings given in the MSA. In the event of any conflict between this SOW and the MSA, the MSA shall control except for matters expressly varied herein and clearly identified as such.

## 1. Background and Scope

Customer is migrating a portion of its on-premises analytics workload to a public-cloud environment as part of its 2026 infrastructure modernization program. Provider has been engaged to deliver Phase 1 of this migration, comprising landing-zone establishment, baseline workload migration, and operational handover, as further described in this SOW.

## 2. Services and Deliverables

Provider shall perform the following services and produce the following deliverables:

| Deliverable | Description | Acceptance Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| D1 — Landing Zone Architecture | Reference architecture for the cloud landing zone, including identity, networking, security baselines, and tagging policy | Approval by Customer's Cloud Architecture Review Board |
| D2 — Migration Runbooks | Documented runbooks for migrating each in-scope workload (twelve workloads identified in Appendix A of the MSA program plan) | Walk-through and sign-off by Customer's Lead Architect |
| D3 — Migrated Workloads | Twelve workloads operational in the new landing zone, monitoring enabled, runbooks updated for the as-built state | All workloads pass Customer's User Acceptance Test (UAT) checklist; production-cutover sign-off |
| D4 — Operational Handover | Knowledge transfer to Customer's operations team, including two (2) workshops and final as-built documentation | Customer's Operations Manager confirms readiness in writing |

## 3. Schedule

Provider shall commence work on the SOW Effective Date and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to deliver in accordance with the following milestones:

- **M1 — Landing Zone Architecture approved:** May 15, 2026
- **M2 — First three workloads migrated and accepted:** July 15, 2026
- **M3 — All twelve workloads migrated and accepted:** August 31, 2026
- **M4 — Operational Handover complete:** September 30, 2026

The SOW shall expire on **September 30, 2026** unless extended by mutual written agreement. There is no automatic renewal.

## 4. Fees and Payment

Total fees for the services described in this SOW shall not exceed **one hundred eighty-five thousand U.S. dollars ($185,000 USD)**, payable as follows:

- $46,250 (25%) upon acceptance of M1
- $55,500 (30%) upon acceptance of M2
- $55,500 (30%) upon acceptance of M3
- $27,750 (15%) upon acceptance of M4

Invoicing, payment terms, late charges, and dispute procedures are governed by the MSA. All amounts are exclusive of taxes, which shall be the responsibility of Customer except for taxes based on Provider's net income.

## 5. Project Personnel

Provider shall assign a Project Manager and a Lead Architect for the duration of this SOW. Provider shall not replace either of these key personnel without Customer's prior written approval, which shall not be unreasonably withheld. Customer shall designate a Customer Project Sponsor and a Customer Technical Lead.

## 6. Change Control

Any change to the scope, deliverables, schedule, or fees described in this SOW shall be documented in a written Change Order signed by an authorized representative of each Party. Provider shall not be obligated to perform any out-of-scope work absent a fully executed Change Order. Either Party may propose a Change Order at any time, and the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to reach agreement.

## 7. Acceptance

Customer shall have ten (10) business days from delivery of each deliverable to either accept it or provide written notice of any deficiencies relative to the acceptance criteria stated in Section 2. If Customer does not provide such notice within the ten-business-day period, the deliverable shall be deemed accepted. Provider shall correct any properly noticed deficiencies and resubmit within ten (10) business days, after which the acceptance period resets. After three (3) cycles of rejection on a single deliverable, the dispute escalation procedure in the MSA shall apply.

## 8. Confidentiality of Project Materials

The migration runbooks, security baseline documentation, and credentials provisioning artifacts produced under this SOW are Customer Confidential Information under the MSA and shall be handled in accordance with the confidentiality provisions therein. Provider shall not use any such materials for any purpose other than performance of this SOW and shall destroy or return them upon completion or termination of this SOW in accordance with the MSA.

## 9. Incorporation of MSA Provisions

The provisions of the MSA governing indemnification, limitation of liability, governing law, audit rights, termination for cause, dispute resolution, and survival are hereby incorporated into this SOW by reference and shall apply with the same force and effect as if set forth herein in their entirety.
