# Analytics Services Agreement

This Analytics Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of June 15, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Quantum Analytics Inc.**, a Massachusetts corporation ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on June 14, 2027.

## 1. Services

Provider shall deliver data analytics services as described in mutually executed Statements of Work.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party from third-party claims arising out of breach of this Agreement, gross negligence or willful misconduct, or any third-party intellectual property infringement claim relating to the Indemnifying Party's deliverables (subject to the standard exclusions for modifications, combinations, and out-of-scope use). The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party, grant sole control of defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section is the sole remedy for IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA. Each Party's total cumulative liability shall not exceed the greater of (A) amounts paid by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential. The Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely to perform its obligations hereunder, protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance and no less than reasonable care, restrict access to those with a need to know, and not disclose to third parties without the Discloser's prior written consent. Standard exclusions apply (already known, publicly available, third-party rightful, independently developed). Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to the Discloser. **The obligations in this Section shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years, after which all confidentiality obligations terminate, including with respect to trade secrets.**

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days (no cure for confidentiality, IP, or late payment), becomes insolvent or enters bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases business. Upon termination, surviving sections survive, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date. This Agreement shall not automatically renew.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive relief to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor) may, on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and not more than once in any twelve (12) month period, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing limitations do not apply.
