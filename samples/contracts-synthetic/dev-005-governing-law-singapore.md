# International Distribution Agreement

This International Distribution Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of August 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Pacific Trade Group Pte. Ltd.**, a Singapore private company limited by shares ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on July 31, 2028.

## 1. Services

Provider shall distribute Customer's products in the Asia-Pacific region as described in mutually executed Distribution Orders.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party from third-party claims arising out of breach of this Agreement, gross negligence or willful misconduct, or any third-party intellectual property infringement claim relating to the Indemnifying Party's deliverables (subject to the standard exclusions for modifications, combinations, and out-of-scope use). The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party, grant sole control of defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section is the sole remedy for IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA. Each Party's total cumulative liability shall not exceed the greater of (A) amounts paid by Customer in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

The Recipient shall use the Discloser's Confidential Information solely to perform hereunder, protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance and no less than reasonable care, restrict access to those with a need to know, and not disclose to third parties without prior written consent. Standard exclusions apply. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to the Discloser. Obligations survive five (5) years; trade-secret protection is perpetual.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days (no cure for confidentiality, IP, or late payment), becomes insolvent or enters bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases business. Upon termination, surviving sections survive, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date. This Agreement shall not automatically renew.

## 7. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

**This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of Singapore, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity, or termination, shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration administered by the **Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC)** in accordance with the SIAC Rules then in force. The seat of arbitration shall be Singapore. The tribunal shall consist of three (3) arbitrators, and the language of arbitration shall be English. The award shall be final and binding on the Parties.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor) may, on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and not more than once in any twelve (12) month period, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing limitations do not apply.
