# Confidential Disclosure Agreement (One-Way)

This Confidential Disclosure Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of November 4, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Beacon Pharma Ltd.**, a company organized under the laws of England and Wales with its principal place of business at 12 Harley Street, London W1G 9PG, United Kingdom ("Discloser"), and **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 100 Market Street, Wilmington, DE 19801 ("Recipient"). Discloser intends to disclose certain confidential information to Recipient solely for the purpose of evaluating Recipient's potential supply of analytics services to Discloser's clinical operations group (the "Purpose"). This Agreement is one-way: only Recipient has confidentiality obligations hereunder.

## 1. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by Discloser to Recipient in connection with the Purpose, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by inspection, including without limitation clinical trial data, study protocols, regulatory submissions, patient data (de-identified), pharmacovigilance data, manufacturing know-how, supplier and vendor lists, pricing information, financial information, and business strategies. All Confidential Information shall be presumed confidential whether or not marked or identified as such.

## 2. Restrictions on Use and Disclosure

Recipient shall (a) hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence, (b) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever, (c) protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own most sensitive information, but in no event less than a high degree of care appropriate to information of this nature, (d) limit access to Recipient's employees and professional advisors with a strict need to know who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein, and (e) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party, including any affiliate of Recipient, without Discloser's prior written consent. Recipient shall not analyze, reverse-engineer, or attempt to derive any composition or process embodied in any tangible material provided by Discloser.

## 3. Exclusions

This Agreement imposes no obligation with respect to information that Recipient can demonstrate by written records was (a) already known to Recipient prior to receipt without obligation of confidence, (b) publicly available at the time of disclosure or thereafter became publicly available through no fault of Recipient, (c) rightfully received from a third party without obligation of confidence, or (d) independently developed by Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information. The exclusion in clause (d) shall not apply to combinations of features or implementations that are themselves confidential, even if individual elements are publicly known.

## 4. Compelled Disclosure

If Recipient becomes legally compelled to disclose any Confidential Information, Recipient shall (a) provide Discloser with prompt prior written notice so that Discloser may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, (b) cooperate with Discloser's efforts to obtain such protective treatment, and (c) furnish only that portion of the Confidential Information that Recipient is legally required to disclose. The Confidential Information that is so disclosed shall otherwise remain subject to this Agreement.

## 5. Return or Destruction

Upon Discloser's written request or upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, whichever occurs first, Recipient shall promptly (and in any event within fifteen (15) business days) return to Discloser or destroy, at Discloser's option, all Confidential Information in its possession or control, including all copies, extracts, abstracts, summaries, and derivative works thereof, and shall certify such return or destruction in writing signed by an officer of Recipient. Recipient may retain one archival copy in its legal department solely for compliance purposes and electronic copies created in the ordinary course pursuant to automated backup procedures, all of which shall remain subject to this Agreement.

## 6. Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for two (2) years thereafter, expiring on November 3, 2027, unless earlier terminated by Discloser upon written notice to Recipient. Notwithstanding the foregoing, Recipient's obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement shall survive for a period of seven (7) years from the date of disclosure of each item of Confidential Information; provided, however, that with respect to Confidential Information that constitutes patient data, trade secrets under applicable law, or proprietary clinical methodology, Recipient's obligations shall continue indefinitely.

## 7. No License; No Representations

Nothing in this Agreement grants Recipient any license, ownership interest, or other right in or to any patent, copyright, trademark, trade secret, or other intellectual property of Discloser. Discloser makes no representations or warranties as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose of the Confidential Information. Discloser shall have no liability arising from Recipient's use of or reliance on the Confidential Information.

## 8. No Obligation to Proceed

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to enter into any further agreement, to disclose any particular information, or to engage in any business relationship. Each Party reserves the right to terminate discussions concerning the Purpose at any time, in its sole discretion.

## 9. Equitable Remedies

Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause Discloser irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, Discloser shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and specific performance, in addition to any other remedies at law or in equity, without the necessity of posting a bond.

## 10. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, United States of America, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement, except that Discloser may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction worldwide.
