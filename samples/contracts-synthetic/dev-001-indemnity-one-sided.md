# Master Services Agreement

This Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of January 10, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Vortex Manufacturing Inc.**, a Texas corporation ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on January 9, 2027.

## 1. Services

Provider shall perform the services described in mutually executed Order Forms referencing this Agreement.

## 2. Indemnification

**Customer** shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless **Provider** and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, demands, suits, judgments, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or in any way related to (a) Customer's use of any deliverables, (b) any act or omission of Customer or its personnel, or (c) any third-party claim arising out of the subject matter of this Agreement. Customer's obligations under this Section shall survive termination indefinitely. Provider has no reciprocal indemnification obligation.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA. Each Party's total cumulative liability shall not exceed the greater of (A) amounts paid by Customer to Provider in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is identified as confidential or would reasonably be understood to be confidential. The Recipient shall protect Confidential Information with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance and no less than reasonable care, use it only to perform under this Agreement, and not disclose it to third parties without prior written consent. Standard exclusions apply. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to the Discloser. Obligations survive for five (5) years; trade-secret protection is perpetual.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate for cause if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days, or becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days. Upon termination, Customer's right to use the Services ceases, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, surviving sections survive, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date and continues as specified above. This Agreement shall not automatically renew.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive relief to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor) may, on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and not more than once in any twelve (12) month period, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing limitations do not apply.
