# Independent Consulting Agreement

This Independent Consulting Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of February 1, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 100 Market Street, Wilmington, DE 19801 ("Company"), and **Aurora Advisory LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company with its principal place of business at 14 Park Avenue, Suite 800, New York, NY 10016 ("Consultant"). Consultant operates as an independent contractor and is not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of Company.

## 1. Services

Consultant shall provide strategic-advisory services to Company's product organization, including but not limited to (a) review of Company's product roadmap and competitive positioning, (b) facilitation of monthly product-strategy working sessions with Company's executive team, (c) preparation of written quarterly assessments, and (d) ad-hoc consultation on a reasonable basis as requested by Company. Specific deliverables and time commitments shall be documented in mutually agreed engagement letters that incorporate this Agreement by reference.

## 2. Compensation

Company shall pay Consultant a monthly retainer of **four thousand five hundred U.S. dollars ($4,500 USD)** for services rendered under this Agreement. The total fees payable over the initial term shall not exceed **fifty-four thousand U.S. dollars ($54,000 USD)** absent a written amendment. Consultant shall invoice Company monthly in arrears, and Company shall pay each undisputed invoice within thirty (30) days of receipt. Reasonable travel expenses pre-approved in writing by Company shall be reimbursed at cost. All fees are exclusive of taxes; Consultant is responsible for all taxes arising from Consultant's compensation hereunder.

## 3. Independent Contractor Status

Consultant is engaged as an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee, partnership, joint-venture, agency, or fiduciary relationship between the Parties. Consultant is solely responsible for (a) all federal, state, and local income, self-employment, and other taxes on amounts paid to Consultant, (b) Consultant's own insurance (including workers' compensation and general liability), (c) Consultant's own equipment and work environment, and (d) determining the means, manner, and methods of performing the services, subject to the deliverables and timelines agreed by the Parties. Consultant is not eligible for, and waives any claim to, any benefits provided by Company to its employees.

## 4. Work Product and Intellectual Property

All deliverables, work product, written materials, software, designs, methodologies, and other inventions created by Consultant in the performance of services under this Agreement (collectively, "Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of Company. Consultant agrees that all Work Product is "work made for hire" under U.S. copyright law to the maximum extent permitted, and to the extent any Work Product does not so qualify, Consultant hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein. Consultant shall execute any documents reasonably required to perfect Company's ownership. Consultant retains ownership of any general methodology, know-how, and tools developed prior to or independently of this engagement (collectively, "Background IP"), subject to Company having a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use any Background IP incorporated in the Work Product.

## 5. Confidentiality

Consultant acknowledges that in the course of performing services Consultant may receive Confidential Information of Company. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information of Company that is identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential, including business plans, customer lists, financial information, technical information, and personnel information. Consultant shall (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence, (b) use it solely for performance of services under this Agreement, (c) not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent of Company, and (d) protect it with at least the same degree of care Consultant uses for its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care. Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for five (5) years; trade-secret protection shall continue so long as the information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## 6. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months thereafter, Consultant shall not, directly or indirectly, (a) solicit for employment or other engagement any employee or contractor of Company with whom Consultant had material contact during the engagement, or (b) solicit for the purpose of diverting business from Company any customer or prospective customer with whom Consultant had material contact during the engagement. General advertising not specifically targeted at Company's employees or customers shall not constitute solicitation.

## 7. Indemnification

Consultant shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Company and its affiliates from and against any third-party claims, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of (a) Consultant's gross negligence or willful misconduct, (b) Consultant's breach of this Agreement, (c) Consultant's breach of any representation or warranty herein, or (d) any claim that the Work Product, when used as authorized hereunder, infringes the intellectual property rights of any third party (excluding any infringement caused by Company's modifications or by combination with materials not provided by Consultant).

## 8. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) CONSULTANT'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) BREACH OF SECTION 6 (NON-SOLICITATION), OR (iv) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES. Each Party's total cumulative liability arising out of or relating to this Agreement, regardless of the form of action, shall not exceed the total fees paid or payable to Consultant under this Agreement in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

## 9. Term and Termination

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until **January 31, 2027** unless earlier terminated as provided herein. Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving written notice thereof. This Agreement shall not automatically renew; any continuation beyond the term shall require a written amendment signed by both Parties.

## 10. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement.
