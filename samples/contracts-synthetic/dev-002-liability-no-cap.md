# Cloud Services Agreement

This Cloud Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of December 1, 2025 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation ("Customer"), and **Apex Cloud Services LLC**, a Delaware limited liability company ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on November 30, 2028.

## 1. Services

Provider shall provide cloud infrastructure and managed services as described in the applicable Order Form.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party from third-party claims arising out of (a) breach of this Agreement, (b) gross negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) third-party intellectual property infringement claims relating to the Indemnifying Party's deliverables. Standard IP indemnity exclusions apply (modifications, combinations, out-of-scope use). The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party, grant sole control of defense, and cooperate. This Section is the sole remedy for IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

The Parties acknowledge that the Services are provided to support critical Customer operations and that any limitation of liability is impractical given the potential scope of damages. Accordingly, **each Party shall be fully liable for all damages, whether direct, indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive, arising out of or relating to this Agreement, with no monetary cap on liability**. The Parties expressly waive any rights to assert that damages should be limited by foreseeability, mitigation, or any other doctrine that might otherwise reduce a damages award.

## 4. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential. The Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely to perform its obligations hereunder, protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance and no less than reasonable care, restrict access to those with a need to know, and not disclose to third parties without the Discloser's prior written consent. Standard exclusions apply. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to the Discloser. Obligations survive for five (5) years; trade-secret protection is perpetual.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days (no cure period for breaches of confidentiality, IP, or late payment), becomes insolvent or files bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or ceases business. Upon termination, surviving sections survive, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date. This Agreement shall not automatically renew. Renewal requires execution of a renewal Order Form by both Parties prior to expiration.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor) may, on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice and not more than once in any twelve (12) month period, audit Provider's records to verify compliance with this Agreement. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report. Where a security incident is confirmed or reasonably suspected, the foregoing limitations do not apply.
