# Master Services Agreement

This Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of March 15, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and between **Acme Corporation**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 100 Market Street, Wilmington, DE 19801 ("Customer"), and **Northwind Systems Inc.**, a New York corporation with its principal place of business at 250 Broadway, New York, NY 10007 ("Provider"). The initial term shall expire on March 14, 2029, unless earlier terminated under this Agreement.

## 1. Services

Provider shall perform the services described in mutually executed Order Forms referencing this Agreement.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, demands, suits, judgments, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of (a) any breach of this Agreement by the Indemnifying Party, (b) the gross negligence or willful misconduct of the Indemnifying Party, or (c) any third-party claim that the Indemnifying Party's deliverables, when used as authorized hereunder, infringe the third party's intellectual property rights. The Indemnifying Party's IP indemnity does not apply to (i) modifications not made by the Indemnifying Party, (ii) combinations of deliverables with materials not provided by the Indemnifying Party, or (iii) use outside the scope of this Agreement. The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party of the claim, give it sole control of the defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section is the sole remedy for third-party IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. Each Party's total cumulative liability arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall not exceed the greater of (A) the amounts paid by Customer to Provider in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one Party to the other that is identified as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential. The Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely to perform its obligations hereunder, protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information (and no less than reasonable care), limit access to those with a need to know, and not disclose it to third parties without prior written consent. Confidential Information does not include information that was already in the Recipient's lawful possession, is publicly available through no fault of the Recipient, is rightfully received from a third party, or is independently developed. If compelled to disclose by law, the Recipient shall promptly notify the Discloser. Obligations survive termination for five (5) years; trade-secret protection continues so long as the information remains a trade secret under applicable law.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause on written notice if the other Party (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days, except no cure period is required for breaches of confidentiality, IP, or payments more than thirty (30) days past due, (b) becomes insolvent or files bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (c) ceases to do business. Upon termination, Customer's right to use the Services ceases, each Party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information, sections that by their nature should survive shall survive, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days following termination.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date and continues as specified above. This Agreement shall **not** automatically renew. Renewal shall occur only upon a renewal Order Form executed by both Parties prior to the expiration of the then-current term.

## 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of New York, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods does not apply. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive or equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein) may, no more than once in any twelve (12) month period and upon at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice, audit Provider's records, systems, and procedures solely to verify Provider's compliance with this Agreement. Audits shall be conducted during Provider's normal business hours, be of reasonable scope and duration, and shall not unreasonably interfere with Provider's operations. In lieu of an on-site audit, Provider may furnish a current SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 attestation. In the event of a confirmed or reasonably suspected security incident, the foregoing notice and frequency limitations shall not apply.
