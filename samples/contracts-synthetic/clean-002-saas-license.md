## 1. License Grant

Provider grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to access and use the Beta Platform ("Service") in accordance with the applicable Order Form.

## 2. Indemnification

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, demands, suits, judgments, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of (a) any breach of this Agreement, (b) the gross negligence or willful misconduct of the Indemnifying Party, or (c) any third-party claim that the Indemnifying Party's deliverables, when used as authorized hereunder, infringe the third party's intellectual property rights. The IP indemnity does not apply to (i) modifications not made by the Indemnifying Party, (ii) combinations with materials not provided by the Indemnifying Party, or (iii) use outside the scope of this Agreement. The Indemnified Party shall promptly notify the Indemnifying Party, give it sole control of the defense, and provide reasonable cooperation. This Section states the sole remedy for third-party IP claims.

## 3. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR (i) INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, (ii) BREACH OF CONFIDENTIALITY, (iii) GROSS NEGLIGENCE OR WILLFUL MISCONDUCT, OR (iv) AMOUNTS OWED, NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR FOR LOST PROFITS, LOST REVENUE, OR LOSS OF DATA. Each Party's total cumulative liability shall not exceed the greater of (A) the amounts paid by Customer to Provider in the twelve (12) months preceding the claim, or (B) one hundred thousand U.S. dollars ($100,000).

## 4. Confidentiality

"Confidential Information" means non-public information disclosed by either Party that is marked confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the circumstances. The Recipient shall use Confidential Information solely for purposes of this Agreement, protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information of similar importance (and no less than reasonable care), restrict access to those with a need to know who are bound by similar obligations, and not disclose to any third party without the Discloser's prior written consent. Standard exclusions apply for information independently developed, publicly known through no fault of the Recipient, lawfully obtained from a third party, or already known. Compelled disclosure requires prompt notice to the Discloser. These obligations survive for five (5) years following termination, with perpetual protection for trade secrets.

## 5. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement for convenience on thirty (30) days' written notice. Either Party may terminate immediately for cause if the other (a) materially breaches and fails to cure within thirty (30) days of notice (no cure period for confidentiality, IP, or late-payment breaches), (b) becomes insolvent or enters bankruptcy not dismissed within sixty (60) days, or (c) ceases business. Upon termination, Customer access to the Service ceases, each Party returns or destroys the other's Confidential Information, surviving sections survive, and Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days post-termination.

## 6. Term and Renewal

The Initial Term begins on the Effective Date and continues as specified above. This Agreement shall **not** automatically renew. Any renewal requires execution of a renewal Order Form by both Parties prior to expiration.

The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in New York County, New York. Either Party may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to protect its intellectual property or Confidential Information.

## 8. Audit Rights

Customer (or its independent auditor under confidentiality protections no weaker than those herein) may, not more than once per twelve (12) month period and on at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice, audit Provider's records and systems to verify compliance with this Agreement. Audits shall occur during Provider's business hours, be of reasonable scope, and not unreasonably interfere with Provider's operations. Provider may satisfy this obligation by furnishing a current SOC 2 Type II report or equivalent.
