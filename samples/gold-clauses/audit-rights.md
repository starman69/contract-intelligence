# Audit Rights (Approved Standard, US, v1)

Customer (or its independent auditor bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement) may, no more than once in any twelve (12) month period and upon at least thirty (30) days' prior written notice, audit Provider's records, systems, and procedures solely to verify Provider's compliance with the terms of this Agreement, including the Security and Data Protection Addendum.

Audits shall:

1. be conducted during Provider's normal business hours;
2. be of reasonable scope and duration;
3. not unreasonably interfere with Provider's operations;
4. respect Provider's policies regarding safety, security, and the confidentiality of Provider's other customers' information; and
5. be at Customer's expense, except that if the audit reveals a material breach by Provider, Provider shall reimburse Customer's reasonable audit costs and remedy the breach in accordance with the Termination clause.

In lieu of an on-site audit, Provider may satisfy this obligation by providing a current, independent third-party attestation report (e.g., SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001) covering the relevant control objectives.

In the event of a confirmed or reasonably suspected security incident, the foregoing notice and frequency limitations shall not apply, and Customer may conduct an investigation at any time on reasonable notice.

## Risk Policy Notes

- "Once per 12 months" is the standard frequency cap.
- 30-day notice is the floor for non-incident audits.
- SOC 2 / ISO substitution is acceptable and reduces operational burden.
- Removal of the security-incident exception → automatic high risk.
- Removal of cost-shifting upon material breach → high risk.
- Customer-paid audits with no reimbursement-on-breach → medium risk.
