# Non-Solicitation (Approved Standard, US, v1)

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination or expiration (the "Restricted Period"), neither Party shall, directly or indirectly:

1. solicit for employment, hire, or engage as a contractor any employee or contractor of the other Party with whom such Party had material contact during the term of this Agreement, without the prior written consent of the other Party; or
2. solicit, induce, or attempt to induce any customer or prospective customer of the other Party (with whom such Party had material contact during the term) for the purpose of diverting business from the other Party.

The foregoing restrictions shall not apply to:

- general advertising, job postings, or recruiting activities not specifically targeted at the other Party's employees or customers;
- responses to unsolicited inquiries from individuals not solicited in violation of this Section; or
- engagements with individuals whose employment or contractor relationship with the other Party ended at least six (6) months prior to the start of recruiting discussions.

In the event of breach, the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and any other remedies available at law or in equity. The Restricted Period shall be tolled during any period of breach until the breaching activity ceases.

## Risk Policy Notes

- Restricted Period of 12 months is the standard. Periods longer than 24 months will not be enforceable in many U.S. jurisdictions and require Legal review.
- "Material contact" qualifier is mandatory — blanket no-solicit covering the entire workforce of the counterparty is over-broad and frequently struck down.
- Carve-outs for general advertising and 6-month-cooled-off solicitation are non-negotiable.
- Use this clause in: NDAs (especially during evaluation of a hire-around risk), consulting agreements, and supplier MSAs where the counterparty has access to talent or customer relationships.
- This clause is generally NOT applied to one-way receiving NDAs (no employee exposure both ways) or pure license agreements.
