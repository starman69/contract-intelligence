# Return or Destruction of Information (Approved Standard, US, v1)

Within thirty (30) days following the earlier of (a) the Discloser's written request, (b) termination or expiration of this Agreement, or (c) completion of the purpose for which the Confidential Information was disclosed, the Recipient shall, at the Discloser's option, return to the Discloser or destroy all Confidential Information in the Recipient's possession or control, including all copies, extracts, summaries, abstracts, and derivative works thereof, in any medium.

The Recipient shall provide the Discloser with a written certification, signed by an officer or other authorized representative of the Recipient, confirming that all such Confidential Information has been returned or destroyed in accordance with this Section.

The Recipient may retain:

1. one (1) archival copy in its legal department or records-management function solely for the purpose of demonstrating compliance with this Agreement and applicable law; and
2. electronic copies created in the ordinary course pursuant to automated backup or business-continuity procedures, where deletion is not commercially feasible;

provided that any such retained copies shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement for so long as they are retained.

This Section shall apply to all Confidential Information regardless of medium, including without limitation paper documents, electronic files, presentations, prototypes, samples, and any tangible or intangible embodiments of the Confidential Information.

## Risk Policy Notes

- Thirty-day return window is the standard. Shorter windows (e.g., immediate) are acceptable; longer windows (>60 days) require Legal sign-off.
- Written certification is mandatory — silent return is insufficient for audit and breach-investigation purposes.
- The two retention exceptions (legal archive + automated backup) are non-negotiable and must be carved out explicitly.
- Use this clause in: NDAs (mandatory), confidential evaluation agreements, M&A diligence agreements, and any contract where one party shares pre-contractual confidential information.
- This clause is generally NOT applied to long-running supplier MSAs where information sharing is continuous and bidirectional throughout the term — confidentiality survival language in the MSA covers the same concern.
