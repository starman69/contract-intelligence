# Indemnification (Approved Standard, US, v1)

Each Party (the "Indemnifying Party") shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the other Party and its affiliates, officers, directors, employees, and agents (collectively, the "Indemnified Parties") from and against any and all third-party claims, demands, suits, actions, judgments, losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) ("Losses") arising out of or relating to:

1. any breach of this Agreement by the Indemnifying Party;
2. the gross negligence or willful misconduct of the Indemnifying Party; or
3. any third-party claim that the Indemnifying Party's deliverables, when used as authorized under this Agreement, infringe or misappropriate the third party's intellectual property rights.

The Indemnifying Party's obligations under clause (3) shall not apply to the extent the alleged infringement arises from (i) modifications to the deliverables not made by the Indemnifying Party, (ii) combination of the deliverables with materials not provided by the Indemnifying Party where the deliverables alone would not infringe, or (iii) use of the deliverables outside the scope of this Agreement.

As a condition of indemnification, the Indemnified Party shall (a) promptly notify the Indemnifying Party in writing of the claim, (b) give the Indemnifying Party sole control of the defense and any settlement (provided no settlement adversely affecting the Indemnified Party may be made without its prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld), and (c) provide reasonable cooperation at the Indemnifying Party's expense.

THIS SECTION STATES THE INDEMNIFIED PARTY'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE REMEDY, AND THE INDEMNIFYING PARTY'S SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE LIABILITY, FOR THIRD-PARTY INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY CLAIMS.

## Risk Policy Notes

- Mutual indemnification is required.
- IP indemnity exclusions (modifications, combinations, out-of-scope use) are mandatory.
- Notice + control + cooperation conditions are non-waivable.
- Indirect / consequential damage carve-outs reside in the Limitation of Liability clause, not here.
- Deviations of any kind require Legal sign-off; mark `RiskLevel = high` on any clause that materially shifts allocation between parties.
