# Termination (Approved Standard, US, v1)

## Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement, in whole or with respect to any Order Form, for convenience upon thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party. Customer shall remain responsible for all fees accrued through the effective date of termination.

## Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to the other Party if the other Party:

1. materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30) days of receiving written notice of the breach (provided that no cure period is required for breaches of confidentiality, IP, or payment obligations more than thirty (30) days past due);
2. becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors, or has a petition filed in bankruptcy that is not dismissed within sixty (60) days; or
3. ceases to do business or is liquidated.

## Effect of Termination

Upon termination or expiration:

- Customer's right to use the Services ceases immediately.
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information per the Confidentiality clause.
- Sections that by their nature should survive (Confidentiality, Indemnification, Limitation of Liability, IP, Governing Law, Dispute Resolution, accrued payment obligations) shall survive.
- Provider shall make Customer Data available for export for ninety (90) days following termination, after which Provider may delete such data.

## Risk Policy Notes

- Notice period of 30 days is the standard for both convenience and cure. Shorter periods (≤14 days) → `RiskLevel = high`.
- Customer Data export window of 90 days is a hard floor; deviations require Data Protection sign-off.
- "No cure" carve-outs (confidentiality, IP, late payment) must remain.
- Survival list is non-waivable.
